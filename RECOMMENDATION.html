My recommendation · 60-second read

Ship the warm & fast version.
Cut the noise. One confident look.

Five decisions stand between a competent app and one that feels like money your mum — and later, a community — can actually trust. Here's the pick I'd ship, and what each one trades off.

★
One-line verdict
E-Max • warm gold accent • Lucide icons • WhatsApp reminders • cut the fakes.
01
Decision 1 — Brand
What do we call it in the UI?
Pick
"E-Max"
Short, warm, conversational. Use "E-EMAX Enterprise" only in the sidebar footer and legal copy. Every premium fintech splits it this way — Wise isn't "Wise Payments Ltd" in the UI.
You lose a tiny bit of formal weight in exchange for a name your mum and her borrowers can say out loud.
02
Decision 2 — Accent colour
Indigo, gold, or teal?
Pick
Warm gold
Gold reads as cedis, trust, and diaspora-coded warmth. Indigo is generic tech. Teal is cold for a money app. Gold on dark feels like a premium bank app, not a side project.
It's a strong opinion. If it's wrong, it's wrong in a bold way — easier to course-correct than a muddy middle.
03
Decision 3 — Reminders
Push notifications or something else?
Pick
Kill push → WhatsApp
Ghana runs on WhatsApp. A "Remind via WhatsApp" button that opens wa.me/<phone> pre-filled with the polite reminder works on every phone, zero backend, zero permissions. Real push costs weeks for a feature mum may never tap.
No silent background pinging. You rely on your mum's daily habit of opening the app once a morning.
04
Decision 4 — Icons
Emoji, Lucide, or Phosphor?
Pick
Lucide
Cleanest professional line icons, ships with shadcn, used by Stripe-quality products. Keeps us premium. Emoji work but render differently on every OS and look less serious in investor demos. Phosphor is a fine alternative if we want rounder edges later.
One CDN script, ~6 KB gzipped. Trivial cost for a big visual upgrade.
05
Decision 5 — What we cut
Which of the flagged items do we actually remove?
Pick
Cut 5. Keep 2 (soft).
Cut the orphan guide page, the fake push toggle, the stale roadmap PDFs, the "custom interest %" footgun, and the diagnostics panel from the main UI (hide it behind a 5-tap easter-egg on the version label, same trick Apple uses). Keep the witness wizard step but make it skippable. Keep the occupation dropdown but add free-text.
Every cut is reversible. Original files stay in place; the refined app just stops referencing them.
